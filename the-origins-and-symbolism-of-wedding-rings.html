    Medieval matchmaking

    The Middle Ages saw a shift in wedding ring design. Precious stones began to make an appearance, each stone imbued with its own symbolic meaning. Rubies represented passion, sapphires mirrored the heavens, and diamonds, with their brilliance, signified strength.

    Another fascinating trend was the rise of fede and portrait rings. Fede rings featured two hands clasped together, symbolising the union of two souls.

    Portrait rings, as the name suggests, displayed miniature portraits of the lovers, a truly personal touch for a bygone era.

    A tradition matching wedding bands

    The tradition of exchanging identical wedding bands is a relatively recent development. Around the 16th century, gimmel rings became popular. These ingenious rings comprised two or three interlocking bands, with each lover wearing one band during the engagement.

    During the wedding ceremony, the bands were joined to form a complete ring, signifying the unification of the couple. Over time, the concept of matching bands became popular, and by the 19th century, it became the norm for both partners to wear identical bands during the wedding ceremony.

    The modern meaning of wedding rings

    Today, the wedding ring remains a powerful symbol of love and commitment. While the materials and styles have diversified, the core message remains. The circle of the ring represents the unending nature of love, a promise that transcends time. For some couples, the wedding ring is a constant reminder of their vows, a silent whisper of devotion amidst the chaos of daily life.

    The importance of wearing a wedding ring

    The decision to wear a wedding ring is a personal one. There's no right or wrong answer. However, for many couples, the ring is a public declaration of their commitment. It can also act as a conversation starter, sparking questions and well-wishes from friends and strangers alike. Ultimately, the importance lies in the meaning the couple imbues in the ring. Whether it's a symbol of love, a constant reminder of their vows, or simply a beautiful piece of jewellery, the wedding ring carries a significance that transcends its physical form.
