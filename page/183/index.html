Full Name: Joseph Albert Walker Profession: Experimental Physicist, Astronaut and NASA Test Pilot Biography: Joseph A. Walker was the American pilot and physicist that first flew an airplane to space in 1963, a feat achieved in the North American X-15 experimental space plane. He flew this plane three times to over 50 miles. The accepted United States definition for the boundary of space is 50 miles, which made Walker technically an astronaut for these flights.
