    Alexandra Grant is an artist who probes ideas of identity, social responsibility, and translation, among others, through the use of language and text in various media. Based in Los Angeles, she frequently collaborates with other philosophers, writers, and artists.

    Growing up, Alexandra Grant was raised in different countries such as Spain, France, and Mexico. Her work is fueled by the need to understand how the images we see and languages we speak influence how we think and exchange ideas. She also seeks to answer how writers and artists can influence culture in a rapidly growing technology-based world.

    Her early life

    Grant was born in Fairview Park, Ohio, in 1973. Her parents, a political science professor and a Scottish geology professor, were fond of travelling to different parts of the world. Unfortunately, they divorced. The artist's mother got custody of her daughter.

    Following this incident, she lived with her mother in New Mexico. She attended a British day school and later enrolled in a boarding school in St. Louis, Missouri. After one year of boarding school, she and her mother relocated to France, where she joined the International School in Paris.

    After a few years, she graduated with a Bachelor of Arts (Art and History) degree from Swarthmore College in Pennsylvania. She also attended California College of the Arts in San Francisco to study drawing and painting.

    Career

    Alexandra's first solo exhibition took place in 2007. This occurred at the Museum of Contemporary Art in L.A. Some people describe her work as a radical collaboration as she collaborates with writers, linguists, philosophers, and other artists to create most of her artistic designs.

    Some of the amazing people she has worked with are philosopher Helene Cixous, writer Michael Joyce, artist Channing Hansen, and actor Keanu Reeves. She has also worked with her sister, Florence, to facilitate an exhibition at The Archer School for Girls in L.A.

    In 2013, she collaborated with Cixous on a twin series of exhibitions. She was joined by participants who assisted in creating large-scale drawings of Cixous' novel. The novel touched on a couple of themes, the major one being telepathy. Grant and Cixous expounded on their telepathic relationship during a conversation from Mains d'Oeuvres to Nottingham Contemporary in 2016.

    Grant has exhibited her work at numerous galleries, some of them including Honor Fraser Gallery, Harris Lieberman Gallery, Mains d’Oeuvres, Ochi Gallery, Galerie Gradiva, Lowell Ryan Projects, Lora Reynolds Gallery, Museum of Contemporary Art, and Night Gallery.

    She is also a mentor and professor in art workshops in different parts of the world. From 2009 to 2011, she taught at the Art Centre College of Design located in California. She has mentored at seminars in Pacific Northwest College of Art's Distance, Ashesi University in Ghana, Syracuse University, and Cal State Northridge.
